Master's Student in the Chinese Studies Department at Incheon National University, Hansol Kim's research interests lie in authoritarian politics and international relations focusing on China. Hansol's research covers China's Elite Politics, the Chinese Communist Party's cadre management system, Sino-US relations, Sino-Russian relations, and Cross-border cooperation between China and other countries under Belt and Road Initiative.

Hansol has served as a co-editor of "Academy of Chinese Studies China Daily Brief," a faculty publication at INU that compiles and summarizes China-related news from international publications and the state-run media in China. This "Brief" has been distributed to Korean scholars who study China and the Korean journalist group on a daily basis.

Prior to his graduate study, Hansol spent two years in the 1st Landing Division of the Republic of Korea Marine Corps as a sergeant and studied Chinese language and culture, and conducted field research in Jinan, Shandong province for a year as a Confucius Institute Scholarship recipient. Further, he worked in the Center for Political-Military Analysis of Hudson Institute, a Washington D.C.-based think tank, as an Asan Washington Young Fellow.

Hansol has participated in research projects in Center for Political-Military Analysis of Hudson Institute, Korea Institute for International Economic Policy, National Research Foundation of Korea, Asia Risk Monitor inc., and Incheon National University.

His research and training have so far been supported by Asan Academy of Asan Institute for Policy Studies, Headquarter of Republic of Korea Army, Institute for Army Power of Sogang University, Confucius Institute, Academy of Chinese Studies of Incheon National University, and Incheon Metropolitan City.

During his college years, Hansol co-founded Incheon National University Debate Club, and his team and he participated in numerous national debate competitions and won several awards. Also, Hansol has appeared on national and regional television, includes JTBC, TVN, OBS, and BTN. Hansol has written for several publications, including The Diplomat, Korea-China Journal, and China Knowledge Network.

Hansol has bachelor’s degree in Chinese Language and Cultural Studies from Incheon National University. Hansol has traveled 30 cities and counties in China. He is fluent in Mandarin Chinese. Hansol was Born and raised in Incheon, Korea.
